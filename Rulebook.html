Official BBDL Rule Book Fall ‘24

Basic Gameplay Rules:

  • Games are played to 11 win by 2 - no rebuttals
  • Teams will switch sides after one team has scored at least 6 points
  • High/Low to decide who throws first
  • Dye must hit the opposing team's side of the table in order to score
  • Hit table and score is 1 point
  • Hit cup and score is 2 points
  • Hit the table and fall to the ground on the shooting team's side is a field goal worth 2 points
  • Sink in the cup is worth 3 points
  • Dye must be thrown at least 8 feet in the air
  • You must tell Mason and send in the group me when a game will be played - Mason will try to be there
  • Each game must have a scorekeeper - verified by Mason
  • Must use official BBDL dye (found in Mason’s room) and red solo cups on all four corners

Fifas

  • Fifas are worth 1 point each
  • You must ladder (1 kick, 2 kicks, 3 kicks, etc…)
  • The first kick CANNOT come from any part of the body besides the foot, other kicks can come from any part that is legal in soccer

Other Rules:

  • Players own any space that is passed their cups. If opposing players come into that space, blocking without the use of arms is fair game
  • The dye must hit the table, the ground, or the cup before any player can touch the dye after a throw. Failure to do so will result in a warning, doing this a second time will result in the player sitting out for three throws, forcing a teammate to play a 2v1
  • SATURDAY RULES: If you talk to anyone outside of the game enough to delay gameplay that is a warning. If you do this twice you will sit out for three throws.
  • If an attempt to play the game inside the right week hasn’t been made by one team, the team who has been attempting to schedule it will be crowned the victor

Playoffs:

  • Tie break is: 1. Point Differential 2. Points For 3. Game to 7
  • Higher seed throws first throughout entire playoffs
  • Switch sides halfway through all games, for finals, higher seed gets to pick their side for games 1 and 3
  • Top 4 Seeds Get Byes
  • Challenge Rounds: highest seed gets to pick their opponents in order
  • For Semi Finals, there is no reseeding
  • First and Second Rounds are one game to 11
  • Semi Finals are one game to 15
  • Finals is a best of 3 series with games to 11
